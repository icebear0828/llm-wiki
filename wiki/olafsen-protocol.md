The **Olafsen Protocol** is a fictional 2024 networking standard for **low-Earth-orbit satellite mesh routing**. It uses **delay-tolerant networking** (DTN) primitives over the **HORUS-9 physical layer**. Each packet carries a **128-bit Olafsen Identifier (OID)** that encodes both the originating ground station and a hop-count budget. Maximum payload size per packet is **4096 bytes**, and the protocol mandates **end-to-end Kyber-768 encryption** for all traffic.
